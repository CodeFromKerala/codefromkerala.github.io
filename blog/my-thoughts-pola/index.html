Well, if you extend the mathematical framework currently used to outside physical observations, there is no guarentee that it is correct unless rigour is introduced.

For example, the de Broglie matter wave was a simple extension through math which wasn’t explicitly measured at the time. Hell, people probably didn’t even know how to measure it at the time.

Louis de Broglie, was lowkey trying to see where physics and math broke ties with this one. He just took Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence equation (the one we all know) and the Planck Quanta Equation (the one some of us know).

$$ E = mc^2 \qquad E = h\nu $$

Now, de Broglie wasn’t a retard, like some of yall. He knew that these things could be put together. $E$ or energy on both sides so why not equate ?

$$ mc^2 = h\nu $$

since $ c = \nu\lambda $,

$$ mc^2 = h\frac{c}{\lambda} $$

and by cancelling some similar things,

$$ mc = \frac{h}{\lambda} \implies \lambda = \frac{h}{mc} $$

From this, de Broglie concluded that particles should also have some sort of wave nature, since they have a wavelength. This was major big brain stuff. Me personally likey this thought process. Lowkey a de Broglie fanboy at ts point.

Keeping the glazing aside, we can see that de Broglie used math to extend the physical world and gain intuition on what the real nature of particles would look like.

This wasn’t a guarenteed success, cus physics cannot be trusted with rigour.
